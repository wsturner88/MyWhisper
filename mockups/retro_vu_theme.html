MyWhisper — 1970s VU Hi-Fi Mockup

An exploration of what the app might look like if we leaned into the analog-equipment aesthetic that the VU meter already uses. Cream chassis with walnut wood, brushed metal plates, chrome push-buttons, real LED indicators, mechanical toggle switches, and labels that look like they were silk-screened on a 1973 amplifier.

All visuals below are mockups — not yet wired into the app. Three sample pieces are shown: the dashboard, the floating recording indicator, and a fragment of the LLM settings.

What's different about this aesthetic

  • Warm cream chassis with walnut frame — like an old Marantz or Pioneer amp from 1974, sitting in a hi-fi cabinet
  • Brushed metal nameplates with engraved-look type for headers
  • Chrome push-buttons for actions instead of flat buttons
  • Mechanical toggle switches for on/off settings (Start at Login, autostart, etc.)
  • Real LED pilot lights — small glowing dots for status indicators (green = OK, red pulsing = recording)
  • LCD-style readouts — black backgrounds with green/red glowing text for timers and important values
  • Label-tape font — Courier-style monospace for technical-looking labels, like silkscreen on equipment
  • Real VU meter embedded in the dashboard when recording, not just floating
  • Meeting list as a tape catalog — each entry looks like a labeled cassette or reel

Practical considerations

  • Light theme — the cream background is brighter than the current dark mode. Lovely for short visits, potentially fatiguing for long sessions.
  • Best as a "theme" option rather than the only look. Settings → Visualization could become Settings → Theme with two choices: Modern Dark (current) and Vintage VU (this mockup).
  • Some elements should leak in either theme — the floating recording indicator with its real VU meter and LCD timer is gorgeous and arguably should always look like this, regardless of dashboard theme.
  • Performance — CSS-only, no extra dependencies, would render fine in the existing WKWebView.
